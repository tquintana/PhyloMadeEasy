Multiple Sequence Alignment


To create a phylogenetic tree, one must first compare the sequences of interest with each other to obtain a rough estimate of their genetic diversity. This is accomplished through a multiple sequence alignment. Such an alignment is beneficial as it calculates the frequency of nucleotides in each sequential position, which will allow one to visualize the point mutations which occurred over time and therefore the genetic similarity among the sequences.


The most common tools used by bioinformaticians to align multiple sequences are ClustalW and MUSCLE, both of which can be accessed by Biopython, a common module with many functions useful to bioinformatics. It is recommended to use ClustalW when aligning DNA and mRNA sequences, and MUSCLE when aligning protein sequences. For information on how to incorporate Biopython, ClustalW, and MUSCLE into your Python program, use the Biopython Cookbook.
